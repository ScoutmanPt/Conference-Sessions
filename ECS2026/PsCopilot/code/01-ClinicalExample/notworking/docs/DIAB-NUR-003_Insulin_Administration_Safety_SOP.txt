PURPOSE
Insulin is a HIGH-RISK medication. Administration errors can be immediately life-threatening.
This SOP must be followed for ALL insulin administration across the Trust.

INSULIN MUST NEVER be abbreviated as "U" for units on any prescription. Always write "units" in full.

1. THE FIVE RIGHTS OF INSULIN ADMINISTRATION

Two-Nurse Check: All insulin doses MUST be independently checked by two registered nurses before administration.

Right Patient:  Confirm identity using open questions + wristband check (Name, DOB, NHS number)
Right Drug:     Read the insulin name in full -- do NOT assume. Confirm type (rapid/short/intermediate/long-acting).
Right Dose:     Check dose against BG reading and insulin prescription chart.
Right Time:     Mealtime insulin given immediately before or immediately after food starts.
Right Route:    All insulin is subcutaneous (SC) UNLESS specifically prescribed IV in a clinical emergency.

2. SC INSULIN ADMINISTRATION -- STEP BY STEP

1. Check Blood Glucose (BG) before every insulin dose
2. Wash hands. Wear non-sterile gloves.
3. Verify insulin type and confirm correct insulin pen/vial
   - Cloudy insulin: Roll gently 10x, invert 10x to mix
   - Clear insulin: No mixing required
4. Attach new needle to pen device
5. Prime pen: dial 2 units, expel into air until drop visible at needle tip
6. Select dose as prescribed
7. Clean skin with alcohol wipe -- allow to dry 30 seconds
8. Pinch the skin at injection site (abdomen, lateral thigh, or upper arm)
9. Insert needle at 90 degrees (45 degrees if very thin patient)
10. Inject full dose -- hold for 10 seconds before withdrawing needle
11. Do NOT recap needle -- dispose immediately into sharps bin
12. Document dose, time, injection site, and BG reading in patient record

3. HYPOGLYCAEMIA MANAGEMENT

Definition: Blood glucose <4.0 mmol/L

Mild/Moderate (Patient Conscious and Able to Swallow):
  Step 1: Give 15-20g fast-acting carbohydrate:
          - 150-200 mL Lucozade Sport, OR
          - 3-4 glucose tablets, OR
          - 200 mL pure fruit juice
  Step 2: Wait 10-15 minutes, recheck BG
  Step 3: If BG still <4.0: repeat step 1
  Step 4: If BG >=4.0: give long-acting carbohydrate (e.g., plain biscuits, next meal)
  Step 5: Recheck BG 30 minutes later
  Step 6: Document and notify medical team

Severe (Patient Unconscious or Unable to Swallow):
  Step 1: Call for help immediately
  Step 2: IV Access: Give Glucose 10% 200 mL IV over 15 minutes
          OR if no IV access: Glucagon 1mg IM
  Step 3: Recheck BG 10 minutes after treatment
  Step 4: Once conscious and able to swallow: give long-acting carbohydrate
  Step 5: Call 2222 if patient does not regain consciousness within 10 minutes
  Step 6: Complete Datix incident report. Notify Diabetes team.

4. COMMON INSULIN TYPES AT NORTHGATE RH

Rapid-acting (mealtime) -- CLEAR:
  Novorapid (Insulin Aspart): Onset 10-20 min, Peak 1-2h, Duration 3-5h
  Humalog (Insulin Lispro):   Onset 10-20 min, Peak 1-2h, Duration 3-5h

Long-acting (background/basal) -- CLEAR:
  Lantus (Insulin Glargine):  Once daily, SAME TIME each day. Onset 1-4h. Duration 20-24h.
  Levemir (Insulin Detemir):  Once or twice daily. Duration up to 24h.

Mixed -- CLOUDY (must be mixed before use):
  NovoMix 30: Twice daily with meals. Onset 10-20 min. Dual peak.

WARNING: Long-acting insulins (Lantus, Levemir) are CLEAR but must NEVER be confused with rapid-acting clear insulins. ALWAYS read the label.

5. STORAGE
- Unopened insulin: Refrigerator 2-8 degrees Celsius (NOT in door of fridge)
- In-use insulin pen: Room temperature, protected from direct sunlight -- discard after 28 days
- Do NOT freeze insulin -- discard if frozen
